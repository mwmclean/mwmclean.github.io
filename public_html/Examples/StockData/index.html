The following two examples analyze stock data market data from both NASDAQ and the London Stock Exchange using data from finance.yahoo.com .

NASDAQ

Model Description

The stock price is assumed to follow a normal distribution with variance \(\sigma^2_\epsilon\) and mean \[ E[\log(\text{Microsoft stock price}_{i}+120)] = \beta_0+ f_1(\{\text{Intel stock price}\}_i). \]

Real-Time Results

The model is fit as described in Luts, Broderick, and Wand (2014) .

London Stock Exchange

Model Description

The stock price is assumed to follow a normal distribution with variance \(\sigma^2_\epsilon\) and mean \[ E[\log(\text{HSBC Holdings stock price}_{i}+120)] = \beta_0+ f_2(\{\text{Royal Bank of Scotland Group stock price}\}_i). \]

Real-Time Results

As above, the model is fit as described in Luts, Broderick, and Wand (2014) .

This data will be updated every ten seconds.
